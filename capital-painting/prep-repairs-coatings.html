Standards

What “prep-first” actually means

  1. Protect surrounding areas and finishes
  2. Repair defects that affect the final look
  3. Prime correctly for adhesion and uniformity
  4. Apply the right system for durability

What you’ll notice

  • Smoother surfaces and cleaner transitions
  • Better adhesion and fewer failures
  • More consistent sheen/coverage
  • Longer-lasting finish
Why is prep such a big deal?

Prep impacts adhesion, smoothness, and durability. It’s the difference between “looks good now” and “lasts.”

Do you repair cracks and damaged areas?

Yes—patching and surface correction are part of our prep-first approach (as needed by scope).

When do you recommend specialty coatings?

When the substrate, wear level, or environment requires a more durable or protective system.

Can I send photos for a recommendation?

Absolutely. Photos help us recommend the correct prep and coating approach.
